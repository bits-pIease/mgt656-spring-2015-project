Bits Please Sprint Report #3

February 18, 2015

Mohammed Abul (mohammed.abul@yale.edu)

Henok Addis (henok.addis@yale.edu)

Arthur Klisz (arthur.klisz@yale.edu)

The stories we completed during this sprint include:

  1. Create a stylized homepage
  2. Display events on the homepage in a responsive manner
  3. Add event gifs to event descriptions on the homepage
  4. Create a stylized About Us page
  5. Create a stylized Events page
  6. Implement Google Analytics A/B testing experiments

Since our previous sprint, where we completed all the technical requirements of the website and passed all of the tests, we focused our efforts on stylizing the website and implementing the A/B testing experiments. To stylize our website, we outlined general designs during our sprint planning meeting and worked to introduce a variety of styles before choosing one based on group consensus. Although this strategy was highly unstructured, we believe it allowed us to push our ideas forward as we were constantly reevaluating the best style options and modifying our website accordingly. Apart from stylizing our website, we continued in our efforts to address the project “curveball”. Although we were successful in implementing Google Analytics into our website during the second sprint, we attempted to introduce A/B testing during this sprint. The team faced a challenge in identifying accurate goals for the Google Analytics platform to track the results of the A/B testing. Although we were able to successfully introduce the A/B testing variations, we were unsuccessful in actually capturing the data on click through rates and conversions. We tried multiple goals, but, in the end, we were unsuccessful.

Our current backlog, in order, consists of the following:

  1. Fix the Google Analytics tracking goals to actually capture the data on click through rates and conversions for analysis

Going forward, we want to continue improving our data analytics capabilities. After successfully implementing all of the technical requirements and stylizing our website to our satisfaction, we would like to understand how users interact with our website and introduce improvements accordingly.

Project Retrospective

In retrospect, we are very satisfied with our performance during this project. We began with a strong Sprint 1 in which we completed many of the project’s technical requirements. This allowed us to focus on completing the remainder of the technical requirements and preparing for the curveball in Sprint 2. As a result, we were free to design and stylize our website in Sprint 3 and focus more on introducing advanced testing. Unfortunately, we were unable to gather the data we were looking for from the A/B tests that we implemented and we would have liked to spend a little more time on it. In the end, however, we believe that we were able to handle the pressure well as a team through constant contact in the form of sprint meetings, standup meetings, and messaging platforms. This allowed us to make decisions quickly and collectively and to continually make progress.
