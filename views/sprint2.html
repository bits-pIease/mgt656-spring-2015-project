Bits Please Sprint Report #2

February 10, 2015

Mohammed Abul (mohammed.abul@yale.edu)

Henok Addis (henok.addis@yale.edu)

Arthur Klisz (arthur.klisz@yale.edu)

The users stories we completed during this sprint include:

  1. Add a select element for each of the year, month, day, hour, and \ minute for elements
  2. Add an input element to submit a newly created event
  3. Redirect the user to the detail page for newly created events \ (if submitted form is valid)
  4. Redirect the user back to the form with errors showing (if submitted \ form is invalid)
  5. Allow the user to RSVP for an event on the event detail page
  6. Added Google Analytics to our website

Since our previous sprint, we have worked to finish implementing all of the remaining functionality of our website for our users. Unlike after our first sprint, now our users can 1) create new events by submitting a form for their event and 2) visit an event’s detail page to RSVP for an event. Additionally, we added Google Analytics to our pages. With Google Analytics, we can now track our users. In an attempt to avoid roadblocks and challenges, we held a sprint team meeting via Skype at the start of the sprint and a standup meeting in-person mid-sprint to track our progress, discuss any difficulties that we had encountered, and outline a plan for the “curveball” (seen by the increase in story points in our burndown chart). The team faced a challenge in deciding how to address the “curveball” scenario. At first, we planned to directly implement all necessary features to execute A/B testing and analyze the resulting data from the Google Analytics platform. However, after understanding more about how the Google Analytics platform worked, we decided to first implement the general code or the platform to track visitors, and then utilize Google Tag Manager in our third sprint to track user journey on our website and better understand which visitors are more likely to trigger carrying tags in our A/b testing process. As during our previous sprint, we coordinated our efforts via the Waffle.io board and communicated via Facebook chat. We are now passing 41 out of 41 tests, according to mocha.

Our current backlog, in order, consists of implementing the following features:

  1. Figure out how our traffic breaks down across different segments and how they flow through your "funnel" from landing to donation
  2. Figure out if making the event links on the home page include the word "RSVP" would increase click-through rate
  3. Figure out if we should ask people to "donate" or "support" an event in the the donation link text to increase conversions
  4. Make our website actually look awesome

By the end of the third and final sprint, we will use our newly implemented Google Analytics to track our users, conduct A/B tests related to click-through rates and conversions, and upgrade our users’ experience by improving the design of our website. We also plan to heavily stylize our website to make create the most enjoyable user experience possible. We plan to directly improve our website features and style in response to the user interactions we determine through the Google Analytics platform.
